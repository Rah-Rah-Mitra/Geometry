The following theorem states a remarkable property of the system space L. 
Theorem 5.1 The system space L is a-flat for all a. 
Intuitively, we may understand this theorem as follows. Since each system S 
in L is identified with the "product" of infinitely many independent Gaussian 
distributions with mean 0 as seen in Equation (5.18), the space L is identified 
with the direct product of infinitely many copies of the l-dimensional statistical 
model consisting of Gaussian distributions with mean 0 which are parameterized 
by the variance alone. The Q-connection on L is then the direct product of 
the Q-connections on these l-dimensional component models. On the other 
hand, every l-dimensional manifold is flat with respect to an arbitrary affine 
connection as noted at the end of §1.7. Therefore L is Q-flat.
