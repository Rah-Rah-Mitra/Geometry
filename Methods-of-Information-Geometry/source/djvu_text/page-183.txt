From the invariance shown in Theorem 8.1, we see that the scalar curvature 
of the Fisher metric is constant on S. In particular, every 2-dimensional  
statistical model which admits a group structure, a location-scale model for instance, 
turns out to be a space of constant curvature (see §8.4) with respect to (the 
Riemannian connection of) the Fisher metric. The corresponding result in the 
case of normal distributions was found by Amari in 1959 by means of a direct 
calculation.
