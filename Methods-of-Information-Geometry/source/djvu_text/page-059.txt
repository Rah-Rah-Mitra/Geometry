2. GEOMETRIC STRUCTURE OF STATISTICAL MODELS 
It should be noted that, unlike Theorem 2.5, Theorem 2.4 has no Q-version 
because a-family is not a-flat in general. What then makes the cases a = ±1 
special? It is easy to answer the question for the case a = — 1. Indeed, this 
simply means that a (—l)-family S inherits the (—l)-flatness from S due to 
Theorem 2.9. The speciality of the case a = 1 can also be understood through 
Theorem 2.9 with the aid of the duality between (±l)-connections. We will 
revisit this in §3.5.
