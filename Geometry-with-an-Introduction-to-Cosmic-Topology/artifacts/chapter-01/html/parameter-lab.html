An Invitation to Geometry: parameter lab

See geometry as the interplay between local measurements and global shape, using flat tori, spheres, cones, saddles, and Klein's invariant viewpoint.

Visual inspection targets

  • Flat torus edge identification: Opposite edges are the same place, not walls.
  • Spherical triangle angle excess: Great-circle edges make a triangle whose angle sum exceeds pi.
  • Three parallel-postulate worlds: One, many, or zero parallels through a point.
  • Cone and saddle sector defect: Sector angle controls circumference and triangle angle sum.
  • Hexagon edge gluing: Corner totals explain when a glued polygon is homogeneous.
  • Erlangen invariant filter: The allowed transformations decide which features survive.
