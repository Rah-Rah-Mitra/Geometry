Geometry: parameter lab

Turn the definition of a geometry into an inspectable workflow: choose transformations, compute orbits, identify invariants, and test homogeneity and isotropy.

Visual inspection targets

  • Erlangen filter: A measurement counts only if the group preserves it.
  • Finite rotation orbits: A seed point generates its congruence class.
  • Reflection geometry: Fixed points and mirror pairs reveal orbit structure.
  • Euclidean invariants: Pairwise distances and angles survive rotations and translations.
  • Homogeneity and isotropy grid: Witness transformations expose which groups move points and directions freely.
  • Mobius distance failure: Inversion keeps angles but changes Euclidean distances.
