Geometry on Surfaces: parameter lab

Connect curvature measurements to topology: circle growth detects k, polygon labels classify surfaces, and Gauss-Bonnet ties area to Euler characteristic.

Visual inspection targets

  • Circle growth detects curvature: The second-order deviation from C=2*pi*r reveals the sign of curvature.
  • Scaled X_k models: The disk radius and metric formulas move with k.
  • Angle of parallelism: Distance and curvature determine the limiting parallel angle.
  • Polygonal surface classifier: Boundary labels produce vertices, edges, faces, and orientability signals.
  • Gauss-Bonnet surface map: The sign of Euler characteristic predicts the geometry type.
  • Quotient and Dirichlet domains: Nearest-image bisectors build the domain seen by an inhabitant.
