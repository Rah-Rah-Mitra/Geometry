The Complex Plane: parameter lab

Make complex arithmetic geometric: addition is displacement, multiplication is scale and rotation, division reverses those operations, and equations describe loci.

Visual inspection targets

  • Complex vectors and addition: Addition is a parallelogram; subtraction points from one complex number to another.
  • Modulus and conjugation: Conjugation reflects across the real axis and preserves modulus.
  • Cartesian to polar form: Argument records direction and is periodic modulo 2*pi.
  • Multiplication as rotation and scale: Arguments add while moduli multiply.
  • Oriented angle formula: The quotient of translated vectors measures the turn at a vertex.
  • Complex equations as regions: Equations and inequalities become visible subsets of the plane.
