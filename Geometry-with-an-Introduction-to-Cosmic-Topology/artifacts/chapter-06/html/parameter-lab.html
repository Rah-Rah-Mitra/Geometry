Elliptic Geometry: parameter lab

Learn elliptic geometry by pairing antipodal points, drawing great circles as elliptic lines, and measuring area by triangle excess.

Visual inspection targets

  • Stereographic antipodes: Opposite sphere points become reciprocal plane points.
  • Great circles as antipodal clines: A cline through a point and its antipode is an elliptic line.
  • Projective disk boundary walk: Boundary exits reenter at antipodal points.
  • Elliptic distance and circles: Large-radius circles fold through the antipodal identification.
  • Lunes and triangle excess: Area is controlled by angle excess.
  • Elliptic triangle trigonometry: Spherical formulas become projective disk computations.
